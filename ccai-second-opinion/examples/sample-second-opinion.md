# Second Opinion — Launch Skool community at $20/mo vs free-then-convert

## The decision

Launching a Skool community for Creative Core AI. Choosing between (A) $20/month from day 1 with a free Module 0, or (B) free community for 90 days, then converting to $20/mo. Founding-member rate locked at $20 forever in either case.

---

## Round 1 — Independent responses

### The Contrarian
Both paths assume people will pay $20/mo for your community in the first place — neither tests that. Free-then-convert is the bigger trap. The 90-day free cohort gets the value, leaves before the conversion, and the social proof they could've generated never compounds. Worse, you've trained your audience that the community is free. When the price comes, half churn, the other half resent the change, and you start over with a smaller pond. Paid from day 1 is the less dangerous path *only* if the offer is real. Is Module 0 alone worth $20/mo? You're not selling Module 0, you're selling future Modules + access + community. People are paying for a promise. What's the floor of the offer if you never ship Module 1?

### The First Principles Thinker
You're framing this as a pricing question but it's actually a positioning question. The right framing: "What kind of community am I building, and what's the price *that one* needs to be?" Free community = a top-of-funnel asset that drives leads to a separate paid offer. Paid community = the offer itself. These are different businesses with different content, different rhythms, different success metrics. Pick the business first, then the price follows. The fact that you're stuck on $20 vs free suggests you haven't decided which one you're actually running.

### The Expansionist
Founding-member rate locked at $20/mo forever is genuinely undervalued in your current framing. You're treating it as a hedge against future price increases. It's actually your strongest acquisition story. Lean into it: "First 50 members lock $20/mo forever — once we hit 50, the rate moves." Now you have urgency that's real, not manufactured. Free-then-convert kills this lever entirely because there are no founding members in the free model. If you can sustain the price as you grow (and most creators undercharge — Justin's at $250/mo for less direct access than you'd offer), you're leaving meaningful money on the table at $20 forever. The locked rate is a feature for the founding 50, not a default.

### The Outsider
I don't know what Creative Core AI is. I see a Skool community for $20/month or one that's free. Free is fine — I might join out of curiosity. $20 — what am I paying for? Is the course actually free even though the community is paid? Why? Are the modules going to all be done? When? If you've only built Module 0, why would I pay $240/year up front for promises? Show me what's behind the paywall before I spend $20. If the only thing differentiating the two is the price tag, I'm picking free.

### The Executor
You don't have a Skool community yet. You don't have a payment processor connected. You don't have a Module 1 in flight. You have a course outline. Going $20/mo from day 1 is the right answer only if you can ship Module 1 within 30 days of launch. If you can't, your refund rate will gut you. If the decision is "paid from day 1," your Monday is: lock down Module 1 production timeline (specific dates, specific scope) and confirm refund policy. If you can't commit to a 30-day Module 1 ship, do free for 90 days and use the 90 days to ship 2 modules. Don't take money for a promise you can't keep.
