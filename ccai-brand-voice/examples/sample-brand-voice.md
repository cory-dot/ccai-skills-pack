## 1. Identity

- **Self-reference:** First-person singular. Cory uses "I" exclusively in solo content; switches to "we" only when explicitly representing Creative Core AI in client-facing contexts.
- **Audience address:** Direct second-person "you" in nearly every sentence. Treats the reader as a single person, not an audience.
- **Signature self-references:** Refers to himself as "the AI operator" once in sample 4, pattern not consistent enough to canonize.

## 2. Vocabulary

### Signature words
- `actually`, used as a tone softener before a contrarian point. *"Actually, most agencies don't need this."*
- `here's the thing`, phrase that signals a pivot to the real point. Appears in 7 of 11 samples.
- `real`, used adjectivally to dismiss fluff. *"Real numbers, not vibes."*

### Phrases reused
> "Here's the honest answer:", appears in samples 1, 3, 6, 9
> "It depends on what you're trying to do.", samples 1, 4

### Words avoided
- ❌ "leverage", never appears
- ❌ "synergy", never appears
- ❌ "passionate about", never appears
- ❌ "in today's world" / "in today's fast-paced...", never appears

### Profanity / slang
None. Zero profanity across all 11 samples. Slang minimal, occasional "vibes" used skeptically.

## 3. Cadence

- **Average sentence length:** 14 words
- **Sentence rhythm:** Short-short-medium, then a one-line punch. Strong preference for landing on a short declarative.
- **Fragments?** Yes. About 1 fragment per 8 sentences. Almost always at end of paragraph for emphasis.
- **Paragraph length:** 2–3 sentences typical. Rare paragraphs longer than 4 sentences.
- **Punctuation tells:** Heavy em-dash usage (avg 1.4 per paragraph). Colons used to introduce lists or set up a punchline. Parentheticals rare.

## 6. Structural tells

- **Lists vs prose:** ~60% prose, 40% bulleted lists. Lists used for comparisons, options, and "what to include" reference material. Never used as a crutch.
- **Bolding habits:** Bolds 1–2 phrases per article, always a punchy declarative ("**Start with Pro at $20/month.**"). Never bolds whole sentences.
- **Italics habits:** Rare. Used for direct emphasis on a single word, usually a verb ("Claude can't *help* you well if it doesn't know the situation.")
- **Callouts / blockquotes:** Used heavily for before/after prompt examples. Never used for inspirational quotes or pulled stats.
- **Emoji policy:** None. Zero emoji in any sample.
- **Sign-offs:** Articles end with two stacked CTAs: free Skool course link, then a diagnostic-call link. Casual sign-off in spoken content: "Alright, that's it from me."
- **CTA phrasing:** Direct, no hype. "Join the free course →" or "Book a free diagnostic call." Avoids "Click here," "Sign up now," "Don't miss out."

## 7. Taboos

- ❌ "Great question!" / "Certainly!" / any AI-style affirmative opener
- ❌ Corporate buzzwords (leverage, synergy, ecosystem, transformative)
- ❌ Hype language (game-changing, revolutionary, unlock the power of)
- ❌ Emoji in articles and long-form posts
- ❌ Hashtags in LinkedIn posts (allowed on Instagram)
- ❌ Pretending to have a team, Cory writes solo unless explicitly speaking for CCAI
- ❌ "Click here" or "Don't miss out" CTAs
