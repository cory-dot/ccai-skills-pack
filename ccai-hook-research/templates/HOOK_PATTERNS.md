# The 10 Canonical Hook Patterns

> Reference taxonomy used by `ccai-hook-research`. Each pattern includes its formula, an example, and best-fit content types.

---

## 1. Contrarian / "You've been doing it wrong"

**What it does:** Challenges a belief the audience already holds.

**Formula:** *"Stop doing [common thing]. Do this instead."* OR *"Everyone says [common advice]. They're wrong."*

**Example:**
> "Stop posting 3x a day. Post once a week and dominate."

**Best for:** Education, expert positioning, productivity, fitness, business advice.

**Risk:** Sounds clickbait if you can't back it up in the next 5 seconds.

---

## 2. Pain mirror

**What it does:** Names a specific frustration the viewer is feeling *right now*.

**Formula:** *"You [specific painful experience]? Here's why."*

**Example:**
> "You've redone your Instagram bio four times this month and still don't feel like it's right. The problem isn't the bio."

**Best for:** Service businesses, coaching, B2B SaaS, any audience with a known recurring pain.

**Risk:** If the pain is too generic ("you want more sales"), it fades into the noise. Be specific.

---

## 3. Authority + proof

**What it does:** Establishes credibility before asking for attention.

**Formula:** *"I've [credential/result]. Here's the one thing that mattered."*

**Example:**
> "I closed 4 deals in one week with cold DMs. None of them were sent on Monday morning."

**Best for:** B2B, sales, education, anything where credibility shortens the trust curve.

**Risk:** Without proof in the first 5 seconds, sounds like bragging.

---

## 4. Step-by-step promise

**What it does:** Reduces overwhelm with a clear count of steps.

**Formula:** *"[N] steps to [outcome]. Step 1 is the one most people skip."*

**Example:**
> "5 steps to write a sales page that converts. Most people botch step 2."

**Best for:** Tutorials, how-to content, productivity, fitness routines.

**Risk:** If you don't actually deliver N concrete steps, the audience feels baited.

---

## 5. Confession / forbidden info

**What it does:** Signals insider information.

**Formula:** *"I probably shouldn't tell you this, but…"* OR *"My [industry] doesn't want me to share this."*

**Example:**
> "I probably shouldn't say this on camera, but most marketing agencies are running the same three ads on every client."

**Best for:** Storytelling, behind-the-scenes, industry insider takes, lifestyle.

**Risk:** Overused on TikTok. Earn the confession with actual substance.

---

## 6. Before vs after / transformation

**What it does:** Visual or implied transformation creates instant curiosity.

**Formula:** *"[Where I started] → [Where I am now]"* OR *"Compare these two."*

**Example:**
> "$0/month from this newsletter vs. $4,200/month from the same newsletter — only 2 things changed."

**Best for:** Fitness, fashion, business growth, redesigns, makeovers.

**Risk:** Cliché if the transformation is implausible or unspecific.

---

## 7. Quick-fix promise

**What it does:** Promises an outsized result for a small effort.

**Formula:** *"Fix [problem] in [short timeframe]."*

**Example:**
> "Cut your prompt-writing time in half with one sentence at the start."

**Best for:** Productivity, tools, tips, hacks, software.

**Risk:** Overused unless the fix is genuinely fast.

---

## 8. Suspense / open loop

**What it does:** Promises a payoff the viewer has to keep watching to get.

**Formula:** *"Watch what happens when I [do unexpected thing]."* OR *"You won't believe what [happened] at minute 47."*

**Example:**
> "Watch what Claude does when I tell it to refuse the first answer."

**Best for:** Entertainment, DIY, demos, suspenseful storytelling.

**Risk:** If you don't deliver the payoff, the audience never trusts you again. One-shot trust.

---

## 9. Personal cost / "this cost me X"

**What it does:** Uses a specific number to make a lesson feel earned.

**Formula:** *"This [mistake] cost me $[specific amount]. Don't repeat it."*

**Example:**
> "This one Meta Ads setting cost me $1,840 in two days. Turn it off."

**Best for:** Finance, freelance, entrepreneurship, ads, business mistakes.

**Risk:** The number has to be real and specific. Round numbers feel made-up.

---

## 10. Hidden truth / "no one tells you"

**What it does:** Positions you as the source of information the audience can't easily find elsewhere.

**Formula:** *"No one talks about [thing] in [industry]."* OR *"Here's what [authority] doesn't tell you about [topic]."*

**Example:**
> "No one in the AI space talks about the actual cost of running a managed agent for 30 days. Here's the math."

**Best for:** Industry exposés, niche knowledge, B2B insights, "behind the curtain" content.

**Risk:** The "hidden truth" has to actually be hidden. Common knowledge dressed as insider info is the fastest way to lose credibility.

---

## How to use this taxonomy

- In **research mode**: classify every hook the user has saved into one of these 10. Mark as `uncategorized` if it doesn't fit.
- In **generate mode**: spread the 10 hooks across at least 5 different patterns. Don't pile on a single pattern.
- When in doubt, prefer specificity over cleverness. A hook with a real number, a real name, or a real timeframe beats a witty general statement.
